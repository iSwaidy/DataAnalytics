Exercise 3A - Reviewing Survey Results

=== QUESTION 1: What do you envision yourself doing 5 minutes before class starts each day, to ensure that you are ready to start on time? ===

1.  What conclusion can you reach about the responses to this question?
	
	Most students plan to log in early, review notes, and have their materials ready.

2. Is the default summary or visualization helpful? How could it be improved?

	The default view just shows raw text with no grouping. Categorizing responses by theme (tech prep, mental prep, review) will improve it alot.

3. For questions with an “Other” option, where can you find the actual text of those responses? Do you notice anything that might be tricky about analyzing these?
	
	Responses are worded differently, making almost impossible to count or compare answers without tagging by theme first.

4. For questions calling for a written response, what challenges do you notice about finding trends or patterns in the answers provided?

	Freeform responses require manual reading to find patterns since there is no way to auto group different phrasings.

5. Now that you can see the responses, how might you recommend rephrasing this or question or the response options in a future survey?

	Converting it to multiple choice would make responses easier to analyze.

=== QUESTION 2: Do you have a second monitor connected to your computer? ===

1. What conclusion can you reach about the responses to this question?

	About half the class has some form of second display while the other half does not. Many students may struggle with screen space.

2. Is the default summary helpful? How could it be improved?

	Responses are freeform, there is no clean yes or no. A simple Yes, No, Somewhat multiple choice would have made this much easier to visualize.

3. For questions with an “Other” option, where can you find the actual text of those responses? Do you notice anything that might be tricky about analyzing these?

	Students answered with "iPad", "second laptop", "3 monitors on my PC" etc., which are technically not second monitors, making accurate counting difficult.

4. For questions calling for a written response, what challenges do you notice about finding trends or patterns in the answers provided?

	Students interpreted "second monitor" differently. The data is inconsistent and would need cleaning before any analysis could be done.

5. Now that you can see the responses, how might you recommend rephrasing this or question or the response options in a future survey?

	Ask "What secondary display do you have available?" with options like External monitor, Second laptop, iPad/tablet, None.

=== QUESTION 3: Do you have an external keyboard and mouse? ===

1. What conclusion can you reach about the responses to this question?

	Half the class has an external keyboard or mouse. Many have one but not both. This suggests that this is not a universal setup with students.

2. Is the default summary helpful? How could it be improved?

	A bar chart showing Yes/No counts would work here since most responses are consistent. However, some answers may cause errors.

3. For questions with an “Other” option, where can you find the actual text of those responses? Do you notice anything that might be tricky about analyzing these?

	Some students specified having only a mouse or only a keyboard, which does not fit a simple Yes/No format.

4. For questions calling for a written response, what challenges do you notice about finding trends or patterns in the answers provided?

	Partial answers like "Just a mouse" do not translate to Yes or No. This makes it hard to summarize accurately without manual cleanup.

5. Now that you can see the responses, how might you recommend rephrasing this or question or the response options in a future survey?

	Separate it into two questions. One for keyboard and one for mouse. Each with a simple Yes or No option.

=== QUESTION 4: How do you plan to take notes for this class – in a doc on your computer, with pen and paper, or something else?  ===

1.  What conclusion can you reach about the responses to this question?

	Majority of students plan to take notes with pen and paper, with a small portion using their computer, and few using both or an iPad.

2. Is the default summary helpful? How could it be improved?

	This question had the most consistent responses and a pie or bar chart would be clean and useful.

3. For questions with an “Other” option, where can you find the actual text of those responses? Do you notice anything that might be tricky about analyzing these?

	A few students said "both" or "iPad," which falls outside the main expected categories.

4. For questions calling for a written response, what challenges do you notice about finding trends or patterns in the answers provided?

	This question had the most consistent and easy to analyze responses of all five questions.

5. Now that you can see the responses, how might you recommend rephrasing this or question or the response options in a future survey?

	Add "Both" and "iPad/tablet" as options to capture answers.

=== QUESTION 5: What is one question that you have right now, about this course generally or about a specific topic we’ll be talking about? ===

1.  What conclusion can you reach about the responses to this question?

	Students are curious about tutoring support, how hard the course will be, and how much SQL and Python they will learn, showing both excitement and anxiety about the 	technical content.

2. Is the default summary helpful? How could it be improved?

	No, open text responses have no default visualization.

3. For questions with an “Other” option, where can you find the actual text of those responses? Do you notice anything that might be tricky about analyzing these?

	Several students left this blank or said "no questions yet," which affect analysis.

4. For questions calling for a written response, what challenges do you notice about finding trends or patterns in the answers provided?

	Each response is unique so spotting patterns requires manual tagging by theme before any summary is possible.

5. Now that you can see the responses, how might you recommend rephrasing this or question or the response options in a future survey?

	Keep it open-ended but add a follow-up multiple choice. "What area are you most curious about?" with options like SQL/Python, capstone projects, tutoring, career 	outcomes.
