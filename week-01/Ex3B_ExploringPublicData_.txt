	Data.gov is a U.S. government open data portal that provides free public access to thousands of datasets from federal agencies.

=== QUESTION 7. Search for USDA, and click into the result. Within the USDA datasets, search for food composition. Notice that the top three results are all datasets concerning nutrition information. Try searching the USDA page for nutrition. How does that affect your results?

	"food composition" in USDA returned 17 datasets, with the top results being nutrient databases like FoodData Central and the National Nutrient Database. Searching "nutrition" returned 229 datasets. A 	broader set including school meal programs, SNAP assistance data, and nutrition assistance reports. This shows how terminology choice significantly affects what data you find, and that a narrower 	technical term like "food composition" returns more specific scientific datasets while a general term like "nutrition" casts a much wider net.


=== QUESTION 8. Go back to the results for food composition, and click on FoodData Central. Review the catalog page for this data source, then click on the button for Visit Page. Take
some time to explore the FoodData Central website. ===

a) Try searching for butter, or any of your favorite foods. What kind of results do you
get? What happens when you click on a result?

	Searching for chicken returned a list of Foundation Foods entries broken down by cut and preparation method. Clicking a result shows a detailed nutritional breakdown per 100g including proximates, 	minerals, lipids, and carbohydrates, with min, max, median values from actual lab samples and the year the data was acquired.

b) Go back to the home page. Find Foundation Foods, and click on Downloads. What
kind of data can you find on this page? How big are these files? Where can you find
more information on the data types available?

	Downloads page has dataset types including Foundation Foods, SR Legacy, FNDDS, and Branded Foods, all available in JSON and CSV formats. File sizes range from under 1MB for smaller datasets up to 	several hundred MB for the full Branded Foods download. More information on data types is available through the Documentation section and the Data Type Documentation link the site footer.
